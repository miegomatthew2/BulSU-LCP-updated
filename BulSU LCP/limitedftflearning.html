GUIDELINES ON THE IMPLEMENTATION OF LIMITED FACE-TO-FACE LEARNING (F2FL) AT BULACAN STATE UNIVERSITY

Guidelines on the Implementation of Limited Face-to-Face Learning (F2FL)

The Province of Bulacan has been under the Modified General Community Quarantine (MGCQ) status since October 2020. Based on the guidelines of the Inter-Agency Task Force (IATF), areas under MGCQ may conduct limited face-to-face learning in Higher Education Institutions as long as the IATF protocols are strictly followed.

Commission on Higher Education (CHED) Chair, Prospero De Vera, in an interview conducted last December 1, suggested that HEIs may opt to conduct limited face-to-face classes under specific restrictions and guidelines. The Joint Circular Memorandum No. 01-2021 is a key reference in the implementation of these guidelines.

BulSU’s Commitment to Quality Education

Bulacan State University remains committed to delivering quality education. The Educational Development Office (EDO), under the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs, has drafted the proposed guidelines for implementing limited F2FL for approved laboratory courses that require physical presence.

Flexible Learning Modalities (FLM) used in previous semesters will continue to be implemented alongside the proposed F2FL guidelines.

Parental Discretion and Student Safety

Participation in limited F2FL is not mandatory. The decision to allow students to attend laboratory classes under this setup is left to the discretion of their parents or guardians. BulSU honors and respects this personal choice.

Official Approval

  • Approved by the University Executive Committee during its EXECOM Meeting on December 15, 2020 at the College of Education Library.
  • Approved by the Board of Regents (BOR) during its 1st regular meeting on March 18, 2021.
